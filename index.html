Interesting Facts About Camel

  1. A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back.
  2. Camels are working animals especially suited to their desert habitat and are a vital means of transport for passengers and cargo.
  3. Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food (milk and meat) and textiles (fiber and felt from hair).

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  1. Chameleons mostly live in the rain forests and deserts of Africa.
  2. The color of their skin helps them blend in with their habitats.
  3. Chameleons that hang out in trees are usually green. Those that live in deserts are most often brown.

Interesting Facts About Panda

  1. The giant panda has an insatiable appetite for bamboo.
  2. A typical animal eats half the day—a full 12 out of every 24 hours—and relieves itself dozens of times a day.
  3. It takes 28 pounds of bamboo to satisfy a giant panda's daily dietary needs.
